About the Earth BioGenome Project

Creating a New Foundation for Biology

Powerful advances in genome sequencing technology, informatics, automation, and artificial intelligence have propelled humankind to the threshold of a new beginning in understanding, utilizing, and conserving biodiversity. For the first time in history, it is possible to efficiently sequence the genomes of all known species, and to use genomics to help discover the remaining 80 to 90 percent of species that are currently hidden from science.

A Grand Challenge

The Earth BioGenome Project (EBP) is a moonshot for biology that aims to sequence, catalog, and characterize the genomes of all of Earth's eukaryotic biodiversity over a period of ten years. This international initiative involves 60 global affiliated projects and counting, all sharing the mission of creating annotated reference-quality genomes for approximately 1.8 million named eukaryotic species.

Why Sequence Life?

Genome sequencing provides crucial insights that benefit humanity in multiple ways:

  • Conservation: Genomic data helps identify endangered species and develop more effective conservation strategies
  • Agriculture: Genomics revolutionizes agriculture by improving crop yields and resilience
  • Medicine: Discovery of new medicines and treatments from natural compounds
  • Climate Change: Understanding how species adapt to environmental changes
  • Bioprospecting: Uncovering nature's solutions to complex problems

Organization

The EBP operates as a global network of collaborating organizations, with several key components:

  • Executive Council: Provides strategic leadership
  • Affiliated Project Networks: Projects around the world contributing to the EBP mission
  • Regional Nodes: Coordinating EBP activities in specific geographic regions
  • Committees: Specialized groups addressing scientific, ethical, and technical challenges

These groups work together to ensure high standards in sample collection, sequencing, assembly, annotation, and data sharing.

Progress
